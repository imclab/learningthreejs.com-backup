Performance: Merging Geometry

Oct 5th, 2011

This article is about performance and how merging geometry can improve them. In WebGL, it is important to reduce the gl calls as much as possible. As always, the rules of thumbs is the less data are exchanged between the cpu and the gpu, the better it is for performance.

To measure the performance, the demo is rendering random cubes. My computer is able to display 2000 cubes at 30fps if the geometries arent merged. But if the geometry are merged, it displays 120000 cubes at 30fps. This is 60 times more cubes. Far from negligable!

An example

The demo is directly derived from a three.js example. I just added controls to let the user play various parameters.
